It took a few months, but we finally saw those two purple lines!

It was a demotivating few months. Most of the people I talked to about it said something along the lines of "everyone is different, you can't expect to conceive right away" while simultaneously getting pregnant the first time they tried. It was painfully frustrating when yet another close friend-couple of ours announced that they had successfully gotten pregnant "surprisingly quickly", which doubly sucked because I felt so guilty that my first reaction wasn't joy.

It was suprising how quickly trying but not succeeding made Emma and I feel insecure. We were only one month in and I was convinced I was infertile, and when I told her about it, she confided that she thought there was something wrong with her eggs. Two months of trying and and I was looking up IVF. I was expecting myself to have a little more mental fortitude, but knowing what's in my supplement cabinet (or the fact that I have one at all), perhaps I should have been expecting the panic I started to feel.

But now thats over (hopefully[^1])! A few months isn't a bad wait, and my heart goes out to the people who have been trying for longer.

I took a video of the moment we turned the test over. Both of us felt awkward doing it, but I felt like we would appreciate having it in 15 years. The moment itself was surprisingly underwhelming. I don't think either of us knew how to feel[^2]. Emma recalled how much she cried when her best friend announced that she was pregnant, and wondered out loud why she wasn't crying at her own pregnancy. I just sat down and ate a shawarma.

I think the difference is a cliff versus a hill. When her friend told us she was pregnant, our lives suddenly went from not-even-the-idea-of-nephew to nephew. In our case we had been trying for months, putting in the work---and it was work: I've never had so much utiliarian sex in my life---this positive test was kinda just the next natural step in a long road made of many, many tiny steps towards "healthy child".

Maybe it will feel a lot more special once we start telling people, or once we pass the viability checkpoints. Which reminds me to mark this post as a draft until 12 weeks are up, and to hope no one is watching my repo.

Sorry to my future child, if you ever read this. It still hasn't quite hit me, how much you will change my life.

[^1] First trimester miscairrage rates are shockingly high.
[^2] Of course, I never really know how to feel at things anyways. Never sing happy birthday to me... or at least don't look at me while you do it.
