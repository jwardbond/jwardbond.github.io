## How I'm feeling

The first person we told was Emma's closest friend (and her husband). They asked me how I felt. I replied "I don't know". It was the day after the positive test, so I don't really fault myself for not having my feelings sorted out. I've put a bit more thought into it, and here is the best way I can explain it:

I feel like Emma and I have been sort of pushing this boulder across a flat plain. A stable relationship... a home together... marriage... kids... all things we knew we would pass if we just continued to placidly work at rolling our boulder along. The positive test was like suddenly pushing our boulder over the lip of a small hill, watching as it it slowly gathers speed aand rolls away from us

Basically: there is no going back now! Woohoo!

I've been alternating between ebullience and anxiety this whole week. I am not super worried about the anxiety, as I imagine that "holy shit I am NOT ready for this" is a common thought in every soon to be dad. The negative feelings are pretty evenly balanced out by the occasional full-body electric shocks I get when I imagine what life will be like in a year.

In a bit of live-reflection: Reading that last paragraph back, I can see that it has started to hit me a little bit more. I am both excited and nervous about this. I am getting more excited to be a dad, but miscarriage rates are still super high. I am so so worried about getting my hopes up and having them dashed and broken.

It's like I said, ebullience and anxiety.

## Telling other people

They say not to tell anyone early who you wouldn't want to comfort you if you had a miscarriage. Over the past week, I have realized that Emma has so many more of these people than I do. I am a bit saddened by it, but not surprised. It fits with everything I know about traditional gender roles and male loneliness. I guess I am surprised that evening _knowing_ this, I let it happen to me. I hope you get your mom's ability to share your heart with the people you love. I hope I do too :).

So far we have told Shannon, Emma's best friend. Actually we didn't even tell her, she just seemed to know as soon as Emma stepped out of the car. There was a lot of hugging and crying (quietly, since we were going inside for dinner and didn't want Shannon's parents to know). Shannon stopped mid-hug with Emma to turn and give me a hug too, which I really appreciated.

The next person we told was (Aunt) Sarah. Again, we barely said anything before she figured it out. Maybe it was the fact that we were sitting next to eachother on the video call that gave it away. Or maybe girls are all telepathic. Anyways all I said was "um" and she broke down on the public bus. I got some good screenshots.

I haven't told anyone yet. I sit in my office and work next to all my friends and think "wow they don't even know that I am going to be a DAD". I want to tell them so badly. I want to tell _someone_ so badly. I need some acknowledgement that it is fucking crazy.

I think I will tell Steven, once him and Natasha get the ultrasound back and confirm everything is going smoothly. But then again maybe I won't? I can't imagine how cripplingly sad it would be if it didn't work out for one of us. But he honoured me by telling me early, so I feel like I should do him the honour as well. And it would also be really nice to have someone to go through this all with, since they are only a couple weeks ahead.

## Changes in Emma

This was one of the parts of pregnancy I was stupidly excited about. Poor Emma, but I've always wanted to find out what she would crave, what would make her ry, laugh, gag, etc.

So far, the following has happened

- She craves kraft dinner
- She seems to crave smoothies (I don't think she would say this, but I have seen her make herself like 3 in as many days)
- Her favourite salad (dill dressing, kale, pearl couscous) makes her gag. I have had to eat an INSANE amount of leftover salad as a result.
- She started crying because she was hungry but couldn't eat the jam and didn't want anything else
- She started crying because she really wanted subway ("Just think of the soft bread and yellow mustard")
- She's extremely tired (she's growing your organs!)
- She has low, persistent nausea

The craziest thing I have noticed is that she smells way different. Her whole head and neck smell like a baby: a rich, sweet scent. I looked it up and apparently some people can smell when a woman is pregnant. I need to find other people to test it one! I am pretty sure Emma is also giving off crazy phermones right now because ya.

## Changes in me

Nothing hormonal (or is there? idk). The biggest change for me in the past week was that I have been starting to think about what I want my kid to learn from me. I have been scrolling on my phone, or distracted at work, and thought the parenthood equivalent of WWJD. I've enjoyed the little extra bit of drive this has given me.

It also occured to me this week that we have a truly giant list of things to do before you are born, but that I wasn't anxious about this at all. I am assuming this todo-list anxiety will start growing as the weeks go on.

## Names

I thought of an awesome name for you. "Piper", after a character in a book I am reading (Assassins Quest by Robin Hobb, it's just OK) and after the Organge is the New Black character. Your mom approved, so I won't tell her where it's from.

That is all assuming you are a girl, of course. If you are boy, we don't know yet. "Takashi" will probably be your name in the womb (just like your cousin's was "Walter"), from a longstanding joke between your mom and I, but there is 0 chance of that surviving 1 second after your birth.

Right now, you are "our little sesame seed". Such a fragile thing.

## In conclusion

Things have started to sink in a little more for me _emotionally_, but no major changes to our lifestyle yet. Unless you count Emma not being able to drink freshly squeezed orange juice as "major". Every day, you make yourself a little more known, and you make us a little more excited.
